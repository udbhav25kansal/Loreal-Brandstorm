TOP 10 Q&A QUESTIONS & ANSWER FORMULAS

Prepare for these questions judges WILL ask:

Q1: "How is this different from [similar innovation]?"
Formula: "Great question. [Similar innovation] focuses on [their approach], while we address [deeper root cause]. Our unique advantage is [3 specific differentiators: tech + L'Or√©al synergy + business model]."
Example (Chile): "Existing solutions focus on topical products. We address the behavioral barrier - men falling off routines. Our advantage: (1) Biosensor tech for personalization, (2) Gamification for adherence, (3) Razor-blade recurring revenue."
Q2: "What if consumers don't want [your innovation]?"
Formula: "We've validated with [specific number] users. [X%] said [specific feedback]. Plus, [market trend data] shows [supporting evidence]."
Example (Kluben): "We tested with 500+ users. 78% said they miss social shopping. Plus, global loneliness epidemic (Surgeon General report) shows 1 in 3 people feel isolated. Our solution addresses a real, validated need."
Q3: "Isn't this too expensive to produce at scale?"
Formula: "Initial COGS is [specific $], but at scale [volume] units, costs drop to [specific $]. Our pricing of [$ amount] gives [X%] margin, industry standard for [category]."
Example (Chile): "Initial device COGS is $85, but at 100K units drops to $42. Our $249 price point gives 83% margin, standard for beauty devices (PMD, NuFace similar). Serum refills have 91% margin."
Q4: "Why would L'Or√©al do this vs. competitors?"
Formula: "L'Or√©al has [specific asset] that competitors lack. We integrate [ModiFace/Perso/Brand] which creates [specific advantage]. This is impossible for [competitor] without [what they're missing]."
Example (Kluben): "L'Or√©al has ModiFace AR tech that already exists. We add social layer that competitors can't replicate without rebuilding AR from scratch. Sephora would need 2-3 years and $50M to catch up."
Q5: "How do you know this will work globally?"
Formula: "[Market size] across [regions]. Cultural adaptation needed for [specific elements], but core problem [root cause] is universal. Start [region], then [expansion plan]."
Example (Chile): "$4.2B men's hair loss market across US, Europe, Asia. Cultural adaptation needed for messaging (machismo in Latam, saving face in Asia), but core problem - men falling off routines - is universal. Start US (largest market), then Europe Year 2, Asia Year 3."

Q7: "Who is your target customer specifically?"
Formula: "Primary: [Specific persona with age, location, behavior]. They currently [current behavior] but are frustrated by [pain point]. Secondary: [Adjacent persona] we capture through [expansion strategy]."
Example (Chile): "Primary: Men 28-45, urban professionals, US/Europe, currently spend $180/year on hair products but 67% abandon routines within 3 months. Secondary: Younger men (22-27) prevention-focused, we capture through influencer marketing showing 'start early' message."
