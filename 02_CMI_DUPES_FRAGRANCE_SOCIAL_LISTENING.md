## DUPE MARKET STRUCTURE

### Three Main Clusters:

#### 1. MASSTIGE (33%)
- **Characteristics:** Physical accessibility and brand popularity/credibility
- **Key Brands:** Zara, Ariana Grande, Victoria's Secret, Bath & Body Works, CRZ, RP Adopt
- **Evolution:** -14% vs LY

#### 2. ORIENTALS (27%)
- **Also Known As:** "Dubai Perfumes" or "Middle-Eastern/Arabian/Arabic Clones"
- **Characteristics:**
  - Excellent value for money
  - Distinguished by olfactory quality
  - Superior longevity
  - Refined packaging
- **Key Brands:** Lattafa, ARMAF, Maison Alhambra, Al Haramain
- **Evolution:** +23% vs LY (FASTEST GROWING)

#### 3. EXPLICIT DUPES (23%)
- **Characteristics:** Openly position as inspired by specific perfumes
- **Approach:** Replicate exact notes and/or overall aesthetic
- **Key Brands:** Dossier, ALT, Oakcha, Fine'ry, Aroma Passions, The Dua Brand
- **Evolution:** +8% vs LY

#### 4. CLEAN DUPES (15%)
- **Driven by:** Dossier brand primarily
- **Focus:** "Clean" formulations
- **Evolution:** -16% vs LY

#### 5. PACKAGING COPY (2%)
- **Minimal presence**
- **Evolution:** -30% vs LY

#### 6. MASS (1%)
- **Brands:** La Rive, Figenzi, Suddenly
- **Evolution:** -48% vs LY

**MARKET SHIFT:** Clear trend toward **higher-quality dupes** rather than low-cost alternatives.

## DUPE BRANDS ARE COPYING:

### #1 The Olfaction
To obtain the same scent:
- Same key ingredients
- Sometimes same olfactive pyramids
- Same olfactive family
- Common acceptance of lack of same longevity

### #2 The Concept & Universe
- Vibe (ie: cocooning, warm)
- Landscape (ie: by the fireplace)
- Perfume trend (ie: IT girl, smells like old money)
- Seasonality (ie: fall woody scent)
- Olfactive concept (ie: feminine scent with fougÃ¨re structure)

### #3 The Product Mix
- Name (same keywords or image and font)
- Iconic pack (bottle shape, juice color, accessories like bows)

## PART 2: KEY DRIVERS & BARRIERS TO PURCHASE

### Consumer Drivers (USA) - Why Buy Dupes?

**Primary Motivation:** Same value for less money (59% of mentions)

**Other Key Drivers:**
- **Power of Influence (18%)** - Peer/influencer recommendations, good online reviews
- **Experimentation/Playfulness (18%)** - Try products, trial before investment
- **Trendiness (15%)** - FOMO, viral products
- **Original Not Accessible (14%)** - Discontinued or hard to find
- **Psychological Satisfaction (11%)** - Smart spending satisfaction
- **Good Reviews (11%)**
- **Desire for Change (8%)**
- **Better Composition (6%)**
- **Brand Love (3%)**
- **Trial Before Investment (3%)**
- **Easy Access to Dupe (2%)**

### Consumer Barriers (USA) - Why NOT Buy Dupes?

**Primary Concerns:**
- **Quality Concern (18%)** - #1 barrier
- **Psychological Barrier (18%)** - Not yielding to cheaper options
- **Social Status (17%)** - Prestige of original brand
- **Lack of Performance (11%)**
- **Poor Packaging (10%)**
- **Original Brand Loyalty (7%)**
- **Ingredient Differences (7%)**
- **Lack of Availability (6%)**
- **Bad Reviews (4%)**
- **Poor Consumer Experience (3%)**
- **Sustainability Issues (3%)**
- **Ethical Considerations (1%)**

### Key Differences: USA vs. France

**USA Consumers:**
- More loyal to original brands
- Express more brand love overall
- Focused on performance and packaging quality

**France Consumers:**
- **Stronger bias against dupes** (44% psychological barrier vs 18% USA)
- **2X more concerned** about low quality
- Strong moral concerns about supporting original creators
- Higher focus on sustainability issues (8% vs 3% USA)
- Priority on formula over packaging

## KEY INSIGHTS SUMMARY

### Perfume Dupe Priority Hierarchy:

1. **Same scent for much cheaper** - Primary driver across all products
2. **Dupes clone head notes** and disappoint in lastingness (commonly accepted sacrifice)
3. **Peer recommendations** and influencer comparisons drive adoption
4. **Viral originals trigger viral dupes** - when original unavailable, dupes step in
5. **Consumers review dupes** rating olfactive likeness to originals (Fragrantica trusted)
6. **Packaging mentioned in lower signal** when particularly cheap
7. **Better composition/ethics discussed** - French consumers defending niche creators

### Barriers That Drive Back to Original:

**For Luxury/Premium Products:**
- Social status brought by original
- Packaging differences (especially cheap bottles)
- Sustainability issues (less clean formula)

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Unique concept/universe**
- **Lastingness** (lack of identical drydown)
- **Premium packaging**
- **Brand prestige and legitimacy**

â†’ To make consumers understand brands offer expert, signed, refined compositions

- Highlight expert perfumers' way of composing
- Emphasize unreplicable aspects:
  - Proprietary ingredients
  - Master perfumers' unique signature
  - Confidentiality of formula (hidden secret never revealed)
- Express how exclusive, complex scent structures are harder to copy (Niche+)

### 3. SUPPORT YOUR LONGLASTING CLAIMS
â†’ To dissuade consumers from adopting dupes, commonly known as less lasting

**Actions:**
- Focus storytelling on long-lasting with rational explanation:
  - Choice of specific raw materials
  - Unique qualities
  - Methods of extraction
  - Balanced compositions
- Talk more about **drydown beauty and sillage (#BEASTMODE)**
  - Consumers acknowledge dupes often lack projection, longevity
  - Dupes copy head notes better than drydowns

### 4. HIGHLIGHT YOUR PRODUCTS' QUALITY & CRAFTSMANSHIP
â†’ To make consumers understand why they pay premium price

- Highlight quality of execution (including luxurious pack)

### 5. DEVELOP PRODUCTS PERCEIVED AS DIFFICULT TO COPY
â†’ To make consumers understand they will never find the same thing elsewhere

- Launch **limited editions** to maintain desirability long-term
- Build **exclusive launches in co-creation** with artists/designers
  - Perceived as not easy to duplicate
  - Position product as collector/decoration item
- **Preempt unique ingredients** or association of ingredients with exclusive origin story

### 6. EDUCATE & RAISE AWARENESS ON SAFETY & ETHICS
â†’ To encourage consumers to stop buying cheap products, counterfeit, or dupes

  - Sourcing traceability
  - Local producers' fair remuneration
- **Leverage weight of official social media accounts** & power of communities & influencers
- **Merge efforts between brands** for more impact
- **Co-create content with retailers**
- **Create an industry label** (respectful of safety & ethics)
